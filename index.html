II Reading
30 pts
A. Details & Inferences
Details from the TextPlausible Inferences
London taxi drivers' hippocampus grows because they memorize routes, showing the brain can physically change through mental activity Practicing memory tasks keeps the brain active, while relying on devices may weaken thinking ability
Using GPS reduces brain activity in areas responsible for navigation, meaning people may stop exercising those skills Over-reliance on technology can reduce problem-solving and navigation skills, making the brain less active
B. Technical Terms
Technical TermContext ClueUse in a Sentence
1. Hippocampus Described as part of the brain responsible for memory and navigation The hippocampus helps people remember places and directions.
2. Cognitive skills Mentioned as thinking, memory, and mental abilities Using technology too much can weaken our cognitive skills.
3. Automation Referred to as systems doing tasks automatically instead of humans Automation makes tasks easier but reduces the need for human effort.
4. Brain activity Explained as how active or engaged the brain is when thinking Too much reliance on gadgets can lower brain activity.
5. Mental health Refers to a person's emotional and psychological well-being Excessive use of technology can negatively affect mental health.
C. Key Information
Topic
The effects of human dependence on technology on memory and cognition
Writer's Opinion
Excessive use of technology can reduce brain activity and weaken memory
Evidence
  • London taxi drivers' hippocampus enlarges with memorization of routes
  • GPS use deactivates brain areas responsible for navigation
  • Mental math and memory skills decline when people rely on calculators or reminders
III Post-Reading
10 pts
A. Rhetorical Analysis
1What credentials does the author have?
Terry Brown is a technology writer at IT Chronicles and writes about trends and impacts of technology.
2What possible biases might the author have?
The author might emphasize negative effects of technology because he focuses on critical tech analysis.
3What is the purpose of the text?
To raise awareness about how over-dependence on technology affects memory and cognition; uses examples, studies, and explanations to support the main idea.
4What specific idea challenges or surprises you?
GPS and automated systems can actually deactivate parts of the brain; surprising because technology seems helpful but can make the brain less active.
5Is the style of writing suitable for the intended audience?
Appropriate for general readers; conversational yet informative; not too formal or too casual.
